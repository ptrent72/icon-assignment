Making an omelette

Omelettes are often considered a staple in one's cooking compendium. You'll find omlettes being made in college student's kitchens and in French bakeries. The base is inexpensive and easy to find eggs that many vegetables or meats can be added to. It all comes together in a short amount of time.

PREPARE THE FILLING

First, make sure to finely chop the vegetables and herbs that you want to add to the omelette. If you're adding any meat, make sure it is cooked and chopped as well, and that any cheese is grated or shredded.

PREPARE THE EGGS

Then, crack the eggs into a mixing bowl, add a little salt and pepper if you like, and whisk together.

PREPARE THE PAN

Add a little butter or oil of your choice to a frying pan on medium heat.

GET COOKING

When the pan is hot, pour in the whisked eggs and let them cover the pan. Let them sit for a moment and then add the filling ingredients and fold or flip the mixture when the eggs start to solidfy.
